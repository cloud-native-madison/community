# Cloud Native Madison Community Governance - DRAFT

## Purpose & Core Intent
This document is a working draft to establish a transparent framework for organizing **Cloud Native Madison**. Our goal is to enable sustainable growth, fair decision-making, and a clear path for contributors. This mission is realized through our [Cloud Native Campfire](https://github.com/cloud-native-campfire) series, anchored by our **Chat**, **Circle**, and **Connect** event formats. We prioritize a high-trust, low-ego environment where technologists can engage as peers, sharing messy real-world challenges without the pressure of sales pitches.

> **Spirit of the Document:** This governance is a living set of guidelines meant to uphold the spirit and values of the community. It is not a binding legal contract designed to be exploited via loopholes. It can and will be amended as needed to protect the community.

---

## Guiding Principles
* **Community Stewardship** – Organizer roles serve the CNCF community, not personal interests.
* **Vendor Neutrality** – No single company dominates; max 50% of organizers from the same employer.
* **Decentralized Leadership** – Teams own their domains; leads coordinate, not control.
* **Open Participation** – Clear paths exist for anyone to contribute and grow.
* **Sustainable Pace** – We share the load, rotate responsibilities, and target a sustainable cadence of a maximum of 1 in-person or virtual meetup per month.

## Contributor Ladder

### Attendees
Everyone who participates in our events.
* **Expectations:** Follow the [CNCF Code of Conduct](https://www.cncf.io/conduct/) and respect fellow community members.

### Volunteers
Community members who help with specific tasks or events.
* **How to become one:** * Express interest to any organizer.
    * Join 3 in-person or virtual events.
* **Expectations:** * Complete committed tasks or communicate early if blocked.
    * Follow guidance from team leads and help create a welcoming environment.

### Organizers
The core team responsible for running Cloud Native Madison.
* **How to become one:** * Active volunteers request consideration as an organizer.
    * Be nominated by an existing organizer.
    * Receive approval by supermajority (2/3) vote.

## Shared Venue Awareness (Important)
All organizers share the responsibility of scouting and being aware of potential event spaces. While primary venues will emerge as we grow (which may carry specific constraints, such as attendance requirements or costs), the community must not be limited to a single venue. Organizers must actively build relationships with alternative venues to ensure our monthly cadence can continue uninterrupted if a primary location is unavailable.

## Decision Making
As of 2026, due to the current organizer count of 3, decision-making will follow **lazy consensus** methodology. As our organizer count grows, decision-making will move accordingly and a voting process will be established.

---

## Conflict of Interest & Recusal
Organizers must recuse themselves from voting on any matter where a direct professional or financial conflict of interest exists (e.g., selecting their own company as a paid vendor).
